Interesting fact about the Ali Bedni Bugyal trek

- Twin Grasslands: The trek takes you through two enchanting meadows, Ali Bugyal and Bedni Bugyal. These expansive green carpets offer breathtaking views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks and are a haven for nature lovers.
- Roopkund Connection: Ali Bugyal and Bedni Bugyal were significant highlights on the popular Roopkund Trek. However, the Roopkund Trek is currently banned, making the Ali Bedni Bugyal trek a quieter and calmer alternative.
- Sacred Bedni Kund: Bedni Bugyal is home to the sacred Bedni Kund, a small lake that holds religious significance for the locals. It is believed to be the bathing place of Goddess Nanda Devi during the Nanda Devi Raj Jat Yatra, a major pilgrimage event in the region.
- Cultural Immersion: The trek offers opportunities to interact with the local villagers in Didna and Wan. You can learn about their customs, traditions, and the unique lifestyle of the mountain communities, enriching your cultural experience.
- Spectacular Mountain Views: Throughout the trek, you'll be treated to awe-inspiring views of prominent Himalayan peaks such as Mount Trishul, Nanda Ghunti, Chaukhamba, Neelkanth, Mandani, Mrigthuni, and Kedarnath. The golden rays of the sun casting upon these majestic peaks during sunrise and sunset create a mesmerizing spectacle.
- Natural Beauty and Serenity: The trek allows you to immerse yourself in the serene and untouched beauty of the Himalayas. The meandering trails, dense forests of conifers, and the absence of human-made structures provide a sense of tranquility and a chance to reconnect with nature.

  • Brief Description About the Trek

    Ali Bugyal and Bedni Bugyal are two mesmerizing high-altitude meadows located in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, India. Ali Bugyal, situated at an elevation of approximately 11,686 feet (3,562 meters), captivates visitors with its sublime beauty and panoramic vistas.
    Ali Bugyal is renowned for its vast expanse of lush green meadows that seem to extend endlessly. The vibrant green carpet of grass, set against the backdrop of towering Himalayan peaks such as Mount Trishul and Nanda Ghunti, creates a postcard-worthy view. The meadow is a sight to behold, especially during the golden hours of sunrise and sunset when the sun's rays cast a warm glow on the surrounding peaks.
    At the top of Bedni Bugyal, lies Bedni Kund, a small lake that holds immense religious significance for the locals. It is considered a sacred site and is frequented by pilgrims during the Nanda Devi Raj Jat Yatra, a significant local festival. The meadow is also adorned with the Nanda Devi and Latu Devta temples, which provide insights into the cultural heritage and traditions of the region.
    Bedni Bugyal, situated at a similar elevation as Ali Bugyal, offers a different but equally captivating experience. Nestled amidst the Garhwal Himalayas, Bedni Bugyal enthralls visitors with its verdant slopes and panoramic mountain views. It is a place of tranquility and serenity, where one can truly connect with nature.
    Bedni Bugyal was previously a part of the famous Roopkund Trek, known for its glacial lake and mysterious skeletons. While the Roopkund Trek is currently banned, Bedni Bugyal continues to attract trekkers and nature lovers who are captivated by its natural beauty and tranquil ambiance.
    Both Ali Bugyal and Bedni Bugyal offer a unique and immersive experience in the lap of the Himalayas. Their picturesque landscapes, breathtaking views, and serene surroundings make them sought-after destinations for trekkers, nature enthusiasts, and those seeking a peaceful retreat amidst nature's grandeur.

  • DETAILED ITINERARY


    Day 1: Drive Rishikesh to Lohajung

    Start your journey from Rishikesh and travel to Lohajung. It is a scenic drive through the mountains. You will pass charming villages and enjoy beautiful views along the way. Once you reach Lohajung, settle into your accommodation and rest for the night.

  • Day 2: Lohajung to Didna

    - Trekking distance: Approximately 3.5 kilometers
    - Altitude gain: From 7,624 ft to 8,045 ft
    - After breakfast, begin your trek from Lohajung to the village of Didna. The trail takes you through forests of conifers and rhododendrons, offering glimpses of the surrounding mountains. Reach Didna and set up camp for the night.
    - Check-in at the campsite for the night.
    - Dinner will be provided at the campsite.

  • Day 3: Didna to Abin Kharak/ Khobal Taal via Ali Bugyal

    - Trekking distance: Approximately 6 kilometers
    - Today's trek takes you through the stunning Ali Bugyal meadow. As you ascend, you'll be treated to breathtaking views of the Himalayan peaks. Continue trekking until you reach Abin Kharak or Khobal Taal, where you'll set up camp for the night..
    - As you are walking through the Ali Bugyal, Nanga Ghunti & Trishuli are prominently visible in the East.
    - Check-in at the same campsite for the night.
    - Dinner will be provided at the campsite.

  • Day 4: Abin Kharak to Bedni Bugyal and back to Ali bugyal

    - Distance: 12 km | Duration: 12-16 hours Altitude gain: 11,010 ft to 12,500 ft.
    - After breakfast, trek towards the captivating Bedni Bugyal. The trail winds through enchanting landscapes, surrounded by picturesque views of the mountains. Spend some time at Bedni Bugyal, taking in the beauty of the meadow and the surrounding peaks. Later, return to Ali Bugyal and camp for the night.
    - Bedni Kund, a pristine lake in midst of this meadow is the sight to feast your eyes on! It is in the vicinityof a small temple, where the devotees pay their obeisance during their halt at BedniBugyal
    - As you traverse the winding roads, relish the scenic beauty of Uttarakhand's landscapes and the majestic Himalayas.
    - Take advantage of the journey to pause at scenic viewpoints, capturing the breathtaking views and creating lasting memories.
    - After exploring the area trek back to Ali Bugyal for dinner and have an overnight stay.

  • Day 5: Ali Bugyal to Wan. Drive from Wan to Lohajung

    - Trekking distance: Approximately 13 kilometers.
    - Altitude loss: From Ali Bugyal to Wan.
    - Today's trek takes you from Ali Bugyal to the village of Wan. Descend through the beautiful trail, passing through forests and meadows. Once you reach Wan, you'll drive back to Lohajung. Overnight stay in Lohajung.
    - The bridge on the Neel Ganga is an idyllic location to wash the dirt and grime of the long trek
    - Trees overhang the river and the water trips and falls over boulders in the shade. The water is cool and refreshing
    - From the river it is a short half-hour climb to the ridge above Wan village.
    - The Lohajung valley. There are many routes to the bottom of the Wan village, and all roads eventually lead down to the road junction (Kasar Bagad) near the hydel project. It takes about an hour and half to get down to Kasar Bagad. It is a steep descent. You’ll take a jeep from Wan to Lohajung. It’s a drive of about 45 mins.

  • Day 6: Drive from Lohajung to Rishikesh

    - After breakfast, depart from Lohajung and drive back to Rishikesh. The journey offers a chance to reflect on the incredible experiences of the trek and soak in the beauty of the mountains.
