Interesting fact about the Char Dham Yatra

- Ancient pilgrimage dating back to the 8th century.
- Located in the picturesque Uttarakhand region.
- Four sacred sites: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath.
- Yamunotri and Gangotri mark the origins of the Yamuna and Ganges rivers.
- Kedarnath is dedicated to Lord Shiva, while Badrinath is dedicated to Lord Vishnu.
- Scenic landscapes and majestic Himalayan surroundings.
- Spiritual significance for Hindus seeking blessings and salvation.
- Challenging treks to reach the temples, adding adventure to the pilgrimage.
- Rich cultural heritage and architectural marvels at each site

  • Brief Description About the Trek

    Welcome to the Char Dham Yatra, a sacred pilgrimage that takes you on a transformative journey through the majestic Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India. This pilgrimage holds immense significance for Hindus and attracts devotees from all corners of the globe.
    The Char Dham Yatra encompasses four holy sites: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. Each destination is nestled amidst awe-inspiring natural beauty, offering a unique spiritual experience.
    The journey begins at Yamunotri, where devotees seek the blessings of Goddess Yamuna. Trekking through breathtaking landscapes and natural hot springs, you reach the revered Yamunotri Temple, marking the origin of the sacred Yamuna River.
    Next, the journey takes you to Gangotri, the birthplace of the sacred River Ganges. As you traverse the scenic terrain of Uttarkashi district, you arrive at the Gangotri Temple, where devotees pay homage to Goddess Ganga. The spiritual ambiance and panoramic views of the Himalayas create an ethereal atmosphere.
    Kedarnath, situated amidst the majestic Kedarnath range, is the next destination on the pilgrimage. The trek to Kedarnath Temple is a profound experience, as you traverse picturesque valleys and encounter the breathtaking beauty of the Garhwal Himalayas. The ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva fills pilgrims with a sense of divinity and devotion.
    Finally, the journey concludes at Badrinath, the most significant site on the Char Dham Yatra. The Badrinath Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, stands as a testament to centuries of spirituality and architectural brilliance. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and the tranquil Alaknanda River, this sacred place offers solace and an opportunity for inner reflection.
    Undertaking the Char Dham Yatra is not only a religious endeavor but also an opportunity to immerse yourself in the breathtaking beauty and rich cultural heritage of Uttarakhand. The journey is filled with soul-stirring landscapes, serene spiritual moments, and encounters with the warm-hearted locals who uphold the traditions of the region.
    Whether you seek spiritual enlightenment, a chance to connect with nature, or a glimpse into the ancient traditions of India, the Char Dham Yatra promises an unforgettable experience. Join us on this profound pilgrimage and let the divine energy of the Himalayas touch your soul.

  • DETAILED ITINERARY


    Day 1: Drive to Yamunotri (6,387 ft) - Trek to Janki Chatti (7,050 ft)

    - After an early breakfast, embark on a scenic drive from Dehradun/Rishikesh to Hanuman Chatti, the base camp for the Yamunotri trek.
    - The drive takes approximately 6-7 hours, covering a distance of around 200 kilometers.
    - From Hanuman Chatti, begin the trek to Janki Chatti, which is around 6 kilometers away.
    - The trek takes about 4-5 hours, crossing beautiful meadows, pine forests, and rivers along the way.
    - En route, you can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding snow-capped peaks and the gushing Yamuna River.
    - Reach Janki Chatti, settle into your accommodation, and rest for the day.

  • Day 2:Trek from Janki Chatti to Yamunotri (10,010 ft) - Return to Janki Chatti

    - Start early in the morning and trek uphill from Janki Chatti to reach the sacred Yamunotri Temple. The trek is approximately 6 kilometers long and takes about 4-5 hours to complete. As you ascend, you'll be treated to magnificent views of the lush greenery, waterfalls, and the snow-clad peaks. Explore the temple premises, take a dip in the natural hot water springs known as Surya Kund, and offer prayers to Goddess Yamuna. After spending some time at the temple, descend back to Janki Chatti and rest for the day.

  • Day 3: Drive to Gangotri (10,170 ft)

    - After breakfast, embark on a scenic drive from Janki Chatti to Gangotri, the next destination on the Char Dham Yatra.
    - The drive takes approximately 5-6 hours, covering a distance of around 190 kilometers.
    - Enjoy the breathtaking views of the picturesque valleys, rivers, and snow-capped peaks along the way.
    - Upon reaching Gangotri, check into your accommodation and take some time to relax.
    - In the evening, visit the Gangotri Temple and witness the captivating Ganga Aarti, a devotional ceremony dedicated to the River Ganges.

  • Day 4: Trek from Gangotri to Bhojbasa (12,432 ft)

    - After an early breakfast, start the trek from Gangotri to Bhojbasa, a scenic campsite located along the Bhagirathi River.
    - The trek covers a distance of approximately 14 kilometers and takes about 6-7 hours to complete.
    - As you ascend, marvel at the mesmerizing views of the Bhagirathi peaks, glaciers, and lush meadows.
    - Pass through picturesque villages, serene forests, and riverside trails.
    - Arrive at Bhojbasa and set up camp for the night. Enjoy the serene surroundings and relax amidst nature.

  • Day 5: Trek from Bhojbasa to Gomukh (13,200 ft) - Return to Gangotri

    - Today's trek takes you from Bhojbasa to Gomukh, the snout of the Gangotri Glacier, which is considered the origin of the River Ganges.
    - Begin the trek early in the morning and cover a distance of approximately 6 kilometers.
    - The trail offers stunning views of the glacier, towering peaks, and pristine surroundings.
    - Reach Gomukh and witness the magnificent sight of the glacier and the source of the sacred river.
    - Spend some time in this spiritually significant place before retracing your steps back to Gangotri.
    - Overnight stay in Gangotri.

  • Day 6: Drive to Guptkashi/Sonprayag

    - After breakfast, embark on a scenic drive from Gangotri to Guptkashi or Sonprayag, the base for the Kedarnath trek.
    - The drive takes approximately 7-8 hours, covering a distance of around 240 kilometers.
    - Enjoy the panoramic views of the mountains, rivers, and charming villages en route.
    - Arrive at Guptkashi or Sonprayag, check into your accommodation, and rest for the day.
    - Take the opportunity to visit nearby temples and explore the local surroundings.

  • Day 7: Trek from Sonprayag/Guptkashi to Kedarnath (11,755 ft)

    - After breakfast, begin the trek from Sonprayag or Guptkashi to the revered Kedarnath Temple.
    - The trek covers a distance of approximately 16 kilometers and takes about 6-7 hours to complete.
    - Ascend through scenic landscapes, crossing rivers, forests, and rocky terrains.
    - Along the way, enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding peaks and the Mandakini River.
    - Reach Kedarnath, one of the most sacred Hindu pilgrimage sites, and visit the ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.
    - Seek blessings and immerse yourself in the spiritual ambiance of the place.
    - Overnight stay in Kedarnath.

  • Day 8: Trek from Kedarnath to Sonprayag/Guptkashi - Drive to Joshimath

    - After an early morning visit to the Kedarnath Temple, begin the trek back to Sonprayag or Guptkashi.
    - Descend through the scenic trail, enjoying the beautiful landscapes and serene atmosphere.
    - The trek takes approximately 6-7 hours to complete.
    - Reach Sonprayag or Guptkashi and board the waiting vehicle for a drive to Joshimath.
    - The drive takes around 7-8 hours, covering a distance of approximately 210 kilometers.
    - Upon arrival in Joshimath, check into your accommodation and rest for the day.

  • Day 9: Drive from Joshimath to Badrinath (10,170 ft)

    - After breakfast, embark on a scenic drive from Joshimath to Badrinath, the final destination of the Char Dham Yatra.
    - The drive takes approximately 1-2 hours, covering a distance of around 45 kilometers.
    - Enjoy the picturesque views of the mountains, valleys, and the holy Alaknanda River.
    - Upon reaching Badrinath, check into your accommodation and take some time to relax.
    - Visit the Badrinath Temple, one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus.
    - Explore the temple premises and soak in the spiritual atmosphere.
    - Overnight stay in Badrinath.

  • Day 10: Drive from Badrinath

    - After breakfast, bid farewell to Badrinath and embark on a scenic drive back to Rishikesh.
    - The drive takes approximately 8-9 hours, covering a distance of around 290 kilometers.
    - Enjoy the beautiful views of the mountains, rivers, and charming villages as you descend from the higher altitudes.
    - Along the way, you can make stops at scenic viewpoints or visit the famous
    - Devprayag, the confluence of the Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers, which form the River Ganges.
