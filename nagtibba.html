  • Brief Description About the Trek

    Nag Tibba, also known as the "Serpent's Peak," is a popular trekking destination located in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand, India. Situated at an altitude of approximately 3,022 meters (9,915 feet), Nag Tibba offers a captivating trekking experience and panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan ranges.
    The trek to Nag Tibba is relatively short and can be completed in a span of two to three days, making it an ideal choice for beginners and those with limited time. The trail starts from the village of Pantwari, which is easily accessible from Dehradun. From Pantwari, trekkers ascend through dense forests of oak and rhododendron trees, passing through charming villages and terraced fields.
    As trekkers make their way to the summit of Nag Tibba, they are greeted with breathtaking views of the snow-capped peaks of the Garhwal Himalayas, including Bandarpunch, Swargarohini, and Gangotri. The summit itself offers a panoramic view of the surrounding valleys and mountain ranges, making it a perfect spot to witness stunning sunrises and sunsets.
    The region around Nag Tibba is known for its rich flora and fauna. The forests are home to a variety of bird species and wildlife such as langurs, leopards, and musk deer. The trek also provides an opportunity to experience the local culture and interact with the warm-hearted villagers of the region.
    Camping is a popular activity during the Nag Tibba trek, allowing trekkers to immerse themselves in the beauty of the Himalayan wilderness. The night skies above Nag Tibba offer a mesmerizing display of stars, away from the light pollution of cities.
    Overall, Nag Tibba is a captivating trekking destination that combines natural beauty, adventure, and cultural experiences. Its accessibility, stunning views, and relatively shorter duration make it a favorite choice for trekkers looking to explore the scenic wonders of the Garhwal Himalayas.

  • DETAILED ITINERARY


    Day 1 - Dehradun to Pantwari and Trek to Nag Tibba Base Camp

    - Depart from Dehradun in the morning and drive to Pantwari, the starting point of the Nag Tibba trek (approximately 3-4 hours drive).
    - Begin trekking from Pantwari to reach the Nag Tibba Base Camp, located at an altitude of around 2,800 meters.
    - The trek to the base camp is approximately 5-6 kilometers and takes around 4-5 hours, passing through dense forests and scenic landscapes.
    - Set up camps at the base camp and spend the night there, enjoying the tranquility of the mountains.

  • Day 2 - Nag Tibba Summit and Return to Pantwari

    - Wake up early and start the ascent towards the Nag Tibba Summit, which is situated at an altitude of approximately 3,022 meters.
    - The trek to the summit is around 3-4 kilometers and takes about 3-4 hours. The trail offers panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks.
    - The first checkpoint will be covered upon reaching Tungnath Temple. The second checkpoint for the day will be Chandrashila Top.
    - Reach the summit and soak in the breathtaking views of the snow-capped mountains and the picturesque valleys below.
    - After spending some time at the summit, begin the descent back to the Nag Tibba Base Camp.
    - Trek type: Easy-moderate. Gradual ascent throughout on a well-defined trail.
    - From the base camp, trek back to Pantwari, covering a distance of approximately 5-6 kilometers.
    - Dinner will be served followed by an overnight stay in Chopta Campsite.

  • Day 3 - Pantwari to Dehradun

    - After breakfast, bid farewell to Pantwari and start the journey back to Dehradun.
    - Reach Dehradun by afternoon or evening, depending on the road conditions and traffic.
    - Lastly, we'll board again for Delhi for an overnight journey by the Afternoon.
    - You can spend the remaining time exploring Dehradun, visiting local attractions, or simply relaxing.
