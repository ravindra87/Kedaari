Har-Ki-Dun Trek

  • Trek
  • Har-Ki-Dun Trek

  • Brief Description About the Trek

    Har Ki Dun is a picturesque valley located in the Garhwal region of the Indian state of Uttarakhand. Nestled in the Western Himalayas, Har Ki Dun is known for its scenic beauty, snow-capped peaks, and rich biodiversity. The name "Har Ki Dun" translates to "Valley of Gods," and it holds immense religious significance for the locals.
    The valley is surrounded by towering mountains, including Swargarohini and Bandarpunch, and is a part of the Govind Wildlife Sanctuary and National Park. The region is characterized by lush green meadows, gushing streams, dense forests, and charming alpine flowers that bloom during the summer months, making it a paradise for nature lovers and trekkers.
    Har Ki Dun is famous for its trekking route, which is considered one of the most popular and scenic treks in Uttarakhand. The trek usually begins from a small village called Sankri, which serves as the base camp. From there, trekkers embark on a journey through dense pine forests, quaint villages, and meandering trails, eventually reaching the beautiful Har Ki Dun valley.
    The trek offers breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, including Swargarohini and Jaundhar Glacier. Trekkers also get a chance to witness the local way of life and interact with the warm-hearted villagers of the region, who belong to the indigenous communities.
    Har Ki Dun is not just a treat for adventure enthusiasts but also a significant destination for researchers and nature lovers due to its diverse flora and fauna. The valley is home to a wide range of animal species like Himalayan black bears, musk deer, langurs, and various species of birds.
    Overall, Har Ki Dun is a captivating destination that combines natural beauty, adventure, and spirituality, making it a popular choice for trekking enthusiasts and those seeking tranquility amidst the Himalayas.

  • DETAILED ITINERARY


    Day 1: Dehradun to Sankri

    - Start your journey from Dehradun to Sankri, the base camp for the Har Ki Dun trek.
    - The drive from Dehradun to Sankri takes approximately 8-9 hours.
    - Enjoy the scenic beauty of the route as you pass through picturesque villages and beautiful landscapes.
    - Arrive in Sankri and check into your accommodation.
    - Spend the rest of the day exploring the village and preparing for the trek.
    - Overnight stay in Sankri.

  • Day 2 - Sankri to Taluka to Seema

    - In the morning, drive from Sankri to Taluka, which takes about 1-2 hours.
    - From Taluka, begin trekking towards Seema, covering a distance of approximately 12 kilometers. - The trek takes you through dense forests, charming villages, and along the banks of the Supin River.
    - Enjoy the natural beauty and serene surroundings along the way.
    - Arrive in Seema and set up camps for the night.
    - Overnight stay in Seema.

  • Day 3 - Seema to Har Ki Dun

    - Start your trek from Seema to Har Ki Dun, which is around 11 kilometers.
    -Pass through alpine meadows, pristine forests, and a few small streams.
    - As you ascend, enjoy panoramic views of the snow-capped peaks surrounding the valley.
    - Reach Har Ki Dun, a breathtakingly beautiful valley surrounded by majestic mountains.
    - Trek type: Easy-moderate. Gradual ascent throughout on a well-defined trail.
    - Set up camps in Har Ki Dun and spend the night there.
    - Overnight stay in Har Ki Dun.

  • Day 4: Exploration Day in Har Ki Dun

    - Spend the day exploring the enchanting surroundings of Har Ki Dun.
    - Take a leisurely walk around the valley, soak in the tranquility, and marvel at the natural beauty.
    - Enjoy the stunning views of the Swargarohini and Jaundhar Glacier.
    - You can also hike to nearby viewpoints for a better vantage point.
    - Overnight stay in Har Ki Dun.

  • Day 5: Har Ki Dun to Seema

    - Begin your descent from Har Ki Dun to Seema.
    - Follow the same trail back, appreciating the familiar landscapes and views.
    - Arrive in Seema and set up camps for the night.
    - You can also hike to nearby viewpoints for a better vantage point.
    - Overnight stay in Seema.

  • Day 6: Seema to Sankri and Return to Dehradun

    - Trek from Seema to Taluka, which takes about 3-4 hours.
    - From Taluka, drive back to Sankri.
    - After reaching Sankri, board a vehicle for the return journey to Dehradun.
    - Arrive in Dehradun by evening.
    - Overnight stay in Dehradun.
